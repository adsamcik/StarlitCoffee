Privacy Policy

Effective date: 24 May 2026 · Applies to: Starlit Coffee for Android (all versions)

The short version. Starlit Coffee is a brewing companion built by one person. Your beans, brew logs, photos, ratings, and notes live in a database on your phone — I never see them. The app does not use analytics, advertising, or crash reporting. It connects to the internet for one thing only: looking up coffee-bag information by barcode at Open Food Facts when you scan a bag. Everything else — barcode reading, label OCR, voice transcription dictionaries — runs entirely on your device.

1. Who I am

Starlit Coffee is developed and operated by Adam Samčík (GitHub: adsamcik), a private individual residing in the Czech Republic. I am the data controller for any personal data processed in connection with the app.

Because I am a sole individual, I am not required to appoint a Data Protection Officer under Article 37 GDPR. You can reach me at play@adsamcik.com for any privacy question.

2. Scope of this policy

This policy covers the Starlit Coffee Android app and this website (adsamcik.github.io/StarlitCoffee). It does not cover third-party services you choose to visit from inside the app (for example, opening a QR code's destination URL).

3. Data stored on your device

The app keeps everything you create in a local database (starlit_coffee.db) and a few preference files inside the app's private storage on your phone. This data is not transmitted anywhere by the app itself. It includes:

  • Coffee bags you've scanned or entered — name, roaster, roast date, origin, tasting notes, photo references, barcode.
  • Brew logs — date, recipe, ratings, tasting notes, optional voice-transcription text.
  • Saved recipes — your custom brew presets.
  • Grinder profiles, ratio presets, cup presets, flavour tags — configuration you've added.
  • Bag photos — image files stored in the app's private files/bag_photos/ directory. Photo paths only are referenced from the database.
  • Preferences — onboarding state, enabled brew methods, default method/filter, selected grinder, last-used ratio, input direction, opt-in flags such as QR link explorer.
  • Recent scan-session metadata — short history of the last 10 barcode scans (locally, never uploaded).

Other apps on your phone cannot read this data because Android isolates it inside the app's private storage sandbox.

4. Camera, scanning & on-device AI

When you tap "Scan bag," the app asks for the CAMERA permission. The camera is used only while the scanner screen is open and only for these purposes:

  • Barcode reading via Google ML Kit Barcode Scanning — runs entirely on your device.
  • Label OCR (reading printed text on bags) via Google ML Kit Text Recognition — runs entirely on your device.
  • Optional smart-fill assistance via Mindlayer's on-device machine-learning SDK — runs entirely on your device through a local bound service, with no network calls.

Camera frames are processed in memory and discarded after analysis. The app never streams video, and frames are never uploaded. The only things that may be persisted from a scan are the bag photos you choose to save and the structured fields (text, barcode) that the scanner extracts for you to confirm.

5. Barcode lookup (Open Food Facts)

When you scan a barcode, the app queries the public Open Food Facts database to look up product information. This is the only outbound network call the app makes on its own behalf.

  • What is sent: the scanned barcode digits, the User-Agent StarlitCoffee/1.0 (Android), and (necessarily) your IP address.
  • What is not sent: any account identifier, advertising ID, device ID, your other bag/brew/log data, or any location data.
  • Where it goes: Open Food Facts is operated by the non-profit association Open Food Facts based in France (EU). Their privacy practices are published at their site.
  • How it is used: the response (product name, brand, image URL) is fetched once per scan and shown to you to accept or edit. Nothing is logged to a Starlit Coffee server, because there is no Starlit Coffee server.

6. QR code product links

Some coffee bags carry QR codes that link to roaster websites. The app's "QR Link Explorer" feature is opt-in (off by default). If you enable it and scan such a code, the app fetches the destination URL's metadata so it can pre-fill bag fields for you. The request goes to whatever server the QR code points to; the destination operator can see your IP address and the requested URL. The app blocks requests to private/internal network ranges as a safety measure. You can disable this feature at any time in Settings.

7. Android Auto Backup

Like most Android apps, Starlit Coffee participates in Android's Auto Backup feature. If you have backup enabled in your system settings, Android may periodically copy a subset of the app's private files — your brew database and preferences — to your personal Google Drive backup space (up to 25 MB per app, end-to-end encrypted by your device PIN/pattern/password on Android 9+).

I deliberately exclude your bag photos and internal scan diagnostics from cloud backup (and from device-to-device transfer) via the app's data_extraction_rules.xml. Photos stay on your device only; if you reinstall or switch phones, your brew history comes back but you'll need to re-take or re-import bag photos.

This backup is managed by Google and Android, not by me. Starlit Coffee never accesses your Google account. You can disable backup for this app via Android Settings → Google → Backup → App data (location varies by device). Uninstalling the app and disabling backup beforehand wipes the local data; existing cloud-backup snapshots are retained by Google according to their own policy.

8. Sharing brew cards

When you tap "Share" on a brew, the app builds a short plain-text summary of that brew and hands it to Android's standard share sheet. From there, you choose the destination (a messaging app, a notes app, etc.). The app does not have a built-in social network, server, or feed.

9. SDKs & third parties

The app includes the following libraries that touch user data, and only in the ways described:

  • Google ML Kit (Barcode Scanning, Text Recognition) — on-device only; per Google's ML Kit terms, models bundled with the app do not send images to Google.
  • Android CameraX (Jetpack) — camera plumbing only; no network.
  • OpenCV — local image preprocessing for OCR; no network.
  • Mindlayer SDK — bound to a local AIDL service on your device; no network calls.
  • AndroidX Room, DataStore, Compose, Navigation, Lifecycle — local storage and UI plumbing; no network.
  • OkHttp / Kotlinx Serialization — used solely for the Open Food Facts call described in section 5.

The app does not include Firebase Analytics, Crashlytics, Google AdMob, Facebook SDK, or any other analytics, advertising, or attribution SDK. There is no third-party crash reporter.

10. Legal basis (GDPR)

If you are in the European Economic Area, the United Kingdom, or Switzerland, my legal bases under the General Data Protection Regulation are:

  • Performance of a contract — Article 6(1)(b) GDPR. To provide the brewing-companion service you installed (storing your data on your device, looking up barcodes you scan).
  • Legitimate interests — Article 6(1)(f) GDPR. For limited security measures such as blocking SSRF-style requests in the QR Link Explorer.
  • Consent — Article 6(1)(a) GDPR. Granted via Android's runtime permission prompts for camera and, where required, system-level features. You can revoke this at any time in Android Settings.

The app does not rely on consent banners because it does not process data for advertising, profiling, or analytics.

11. Retention & deletion

  • On your device: data is kept for as long as you use the app. You can delete individual bags, brews, recipes, or photos from within the app at any time.
  • Full wipe: uninstalling the app removes the private database, preferences, and photo files. (Items already copied into Android Auto Backup remain in your Google Drive backup per Google's retention rules — see section 7.)
  • Open Food Facts queries: handled per Open Food Facts' own logs and policies. I do not retain a copy.
  • This website: served by GitHub Pages. GitHub keeps standard request logs for security/abuse-prevention purposes; see GitHub's privacy statement.

12. Your rights

Under the GDPR you have the right to:

  • Access the personal data I hold about you (in practice: it lives on your device; you can read it directly).
  • Rectify inaccurate data — edit bags/brews/notes in the app.
  • Erase your data — delete individual records or uninstall the app.
  • Restrict or object to processing.
  • Portability — request an export of any data I might hold about you (because everything is on your device, an explicit export request is rarely necessary, but you can ask).
  • Withdraw consent — revoke the camera or any other permission in Android Settings.
  • Lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority (see section 18).

To exercise any of these rights, email play@adsamcik.com. I will respond within 30 days.

13. Children

Starlit Coffee is intended for adults brewing coffee. It is not directed at children under 13 (or under 16, where applicable under local law). I do not knowingly collect personal data from children. If you believe a child has used the app in a way that requires action, please contact me.

14. Security

I take reasonable, proportionate measures appropriate to the limited data the app handles:

  • The Open Food Facts call uses HTTPS (TLS 1.2+).
  • App data lives in Android's per-app sandbox, isolated from other apps.
  • QR Link Explorer enforces an allow-list of public IP ranges to mitigate server-side request forgery against private networks.
  • Released builds are signed with Google Play App Signing.

No system is perfectly secure. Please keep your phone updated and lock-screen-protected; that is the single most effective protection for the data Starlit Coffee stores.

15. International transfers

Open Food Facts is hosted in France (EU), so barcode lookups stay within the EEA. If you have Android Auto Backup enabled, Google may store backups on servers outside the EEA; Google relies on the EU–US Data Privacy Framework and standard contractual clauses for those transfers. This website is served by GitHub (a US company) via global CDN.

16. California & other US privacy rights

Starlit Coffee does not meet the CCPA/CPRA business thresholds (no $25M+ revenue, fewer than 100,000 California residents, no sale or sharing of personal information for cross-context behavioural advertising). Nevertheless, if you are a California resident: I do not sell or share your personal information, I do not use sensitive personal information for any purpose, and the rights described in section 12 apply to you in practice. Similar rights exist under Colorado, Virginia, Connecticut, Utah, Texas and other US state laws — they are honoured the same way.

17. Changes to this policy

I may update this policy as the app changes. The "Effective date" at the top will be updated, and material changes will be announced in the app or release notes. The full history of edits is public in the GitHub repository under docs/privacy/index.html.

18. Contact & complaints

If you believe I have processed your personal data unlawfully, you have the right to complain to a supervisory authority. For most users this will be:
