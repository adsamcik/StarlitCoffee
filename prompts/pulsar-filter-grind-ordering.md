# Research prompt: NextLevel Pulsar paper vs metal filter grind ordering

## Context
The NextLevel Pulsar is an immersion/percolation hybrid dripper with a valve
that controls flow rate. It supports three filter types:

- **PAPER** — standard paper filter
- **METAL_19K** — 19-micron stainless mesh (sold as the "bold" filter)
- **METAL_40K** — 40-micron stainless mesh (sold as the "balanced" filter)

I need a definitive answer on **how grind size should change between these
three filters when brewing on the Pulsar**, with recipe ratio held constant
(1:17, 20–25 g dose).

Two competing claims exist:

1. **App's current convention** (PAPER finest → 19K coarser → 40K coarsest).
   Rationale: paper restricts flow most so a finer grind compensates;
   coarser metal-mesh filters need coarser grind to limit fines passing through.

2. **Generic pour-over guidance from web** (PAPER medium-coarse, metal = medium,
   i.e. PAPER COARSER than metal). Rationale: paper traps fines, metal lets
   fines through so finer grind is fine; total contact time is similar.

Which is correct for the **NextLevel Pulsar specifically** (not generic
pour-over, not Hario Switch, not AeroPress)?

## What I want
A short, sourced answer (≤ 250 words) covering:

1. **The correct ordering** of grind size across PAPER / METAL_19K / METAL_40K
   on the Pulsar specifically, holding ratio + valve technique constant.
2. **Why** — mechanism (flow restriction vs fines vs body target).
3. **Concrete delta** if known (e.g. "go ~1 click coarser per filter step on
   a Comandante C40", or "no meaningful change, adjust by taste").
4. **Sources** — prefer in this order:
   - NextLevel Coffee official site (nextlevel-coffee.com), product pages,
     manuals, blog posts.
   - NextLevel founder interviews / podcast appearances (Lance Hedrick,
     James Hoffmann reviews of the Pulsar).
   - YouTube reviews from credible coffee channels (Lance Hedrick, James
     Hoffmann, European Coffee Trip, Coffee Chronicler).
   - Reddit r/coffee, r/pourover threads with Pulsar tags.
   - Home-barista.com Pulsar threads.

## Anti-patterns to avoid
- Don't generalise from "paper traps fines so brew finer with metal" —
  the Pulsar's valve mechanic changes things vs an open-bottom dripper.
- Don't cite generic V60 / Kalita guidance unless the source explicitly
  applies it to the Pulsar.
- Don't fabricate numbers. If no source gives a concrete delta, say so.
